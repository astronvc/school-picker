# Our needs — evaluation criteria

Last updated: 2026-05-04

## Stated priorities

1. **Strong academics** — measurable rigor, university outcomes, real depth not surface
2. **Confidence** — child should leave school self-assured, not anxious or compliance-trained
3. **Competitive** — internationally competitive for top universities and beyond
4. **Independent thinking** — habit of inquiry, not memorization; debate, write, argue, design
5. **Research approach** — projects, investigation, experiment design (extended essays, IAs, capstones)
6. **Strong math and physics fundamentals** — non-negotiable. Want a child who can do real proof-based math and quantitative physics, not just plug-and-chug.
7. **Forward-looking (发展眼光)** — optimizes for skills valued in the 2035–2050 economy, not 2010

## What we want to avoid

- 填鸭式教育 (force-feeding facts)
- 做题 culture (drilling exam questions until reflexive)
- Pure 考试 orientation (school as a test-prep factory)
- Anxiety-driven environments where ranking is the daily metric
- Schools that sand off curiosity by Grade 6

## Tradeoffs we accept

- Higher fees for stronger pedagogy (within reason)
- Less Mandarin immersion if it buys substantially better academics + thinking
- Less Cantonese immersion if it buys international university optionality
- Travel time tradeoff if school is materially better

## Constraints

- **Child holds HK passport**; **parents from mainland China**. This affects:
  - Eligibility for some local government / DSS schools (POA via residence)
  - Debenture/nomination dynamics at international schools (corporate vs individual)
  - Mandarin/Cantonese/English language exposure preferences
  - Path to mainland universities (Joint Entrance Exam for Hong Kong, Macao and Taiwan students)
- **Budget** — to be filled in by user (HKD/year per child + debenture if applicable)
- **Location** — to be filled in by user (district/area to limit commute)

## Decision dimensions (used in scorecard)

| # | Dimension | What "high score" looks like |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Academic rigor | Strong external exam outcomes (IB ≥ 38, AP 5s, A* in A-Level), top university destinations |
| 2 | Math / physics depth | Has IB HL Math AA, Further Math, AP Physics C, A-Level Further Math, or equivalent rigour. Olympiad culture a plus. |
| 3 | Independent thinking | EE / IA / capstone / Harkness / project-based; visible student work and debate |
| 4 | Confidence-building culture | Pastoral care quality, low anxiety, healthy peer dynamics |
| 5 | Future-skill alignment | AI literacy, complex problem solving, communication, cross-cultural fluency |
| 6 | Admissions feasibility for our family | Realistic entry given passport / language / years in HK / sibling status |
| 7 | University outcomes | Top-50 globally placement rates + diversity of paths (US/UK/HK/mainland) |
| 8 | Language profile | English-medium with serious Mandarin and ideally some Cantonese |
| 9 | Cost / value | Fees + capital levy + debenture in context of academic value delivered |
| 10 | Community / peer group | Quality of peer cohort and parent body (motivation, ambition, character) |
