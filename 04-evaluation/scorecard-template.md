# Scorecard template

Last updated: 2026-05-04

Use this template to score any candidate school. Each dimension is weighted by what matters most to the family. Rationale > number — the score is a tool to expose tradeoffs, not the answer itself.

## Weights

| # | Dimension | Weight | Notes |
|---|---|---:|---|
| 1 | Academic rigor | 15% | External exam outcomes, depth of curriculum |
| 2 | Math / physics depth | 15% | Real proof + quantitative depth, not coverage |
| 3 | Independent thinking | 12% | EE/IA/capstone, debate, project work |
| 4 | Confidence-building culture | 10% | Pastoral care, low anxiety, healthy peers |
| 5 | Future-skill alignment | 10% | Agency, taste, AI-era literacy |
| 6 | Admissions feasibility | 10% | Realistic for HK passport child + mainland parents |
| 7 | University outcomes | 10% | Top-50 globally + diversity of paths |
| 8 | Language profile | 8% | EMI + Mandarin + (some Cantonese) |
| 9 | Cost / value | 5% | Fees + capital levy + debenture vs delivered value |
| 10 | Community / peer group | 5% | Quality of cohort and parent body |

(Weights are a starting point. The user should adjust to their actual priorities.)

## Score interpretation

- **4.5+** — Top tier. Pursue actively if admissions feasible.
- **4.0–4.5** — Strong candidate. Worth visiting and applying.
- **3.5–4.0** — Backup tier. Apply if portfolio room.
- **<3.5** — Likely not worth the cost / effort.

The number isn't the decision. Use it to surface where schools win and lose, then make the call against your gut and any factor not captured by the scorecard.
