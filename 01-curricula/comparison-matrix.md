# Curriculum comparison matrix

A side-by-side analysis of **IB DP, AP, A-Level (Cambridge / Edexcel), HKDSE, and IGCSE** against the criteria that matter for this family.

For each curriculum, see the dedicated file in this directory for full detail and citations.

## Math depth — the critical axis for this family

Ranked roughly by depth/proof-rigor for a STEM-bound student:

| Rank | Course | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **A-Level Maths + Further Maths (CAIE 9709 + 9231 / Edexcel 9MA0 + 9FM0)** | Includes complex numbers, matrices, induction proofs, polar curves, ODEs, group theory basics. Closest to first-year university math. |
| 2 | **AP Calc BC + post-AP Multivariable + Linear Algebra** | Calc BC is rigorous; post-AP courses (offered at HKIS) extend depth. No mandatory proof culture but strong calculus. |
| 3 | **IB Math AA HL** | Single-variable calculus + complex numbers + vectors + induction proofs. Genuine but less deep than A-Level Maths + Further Maths. |
| 4 | **A-Level Maths (alone)** | Solid, calculus-rich, less proof-heavy without Further Maths. |
| 5 | **AP Calc BC (alone)** | Equivalent to first-year university calculus I + II. No proof, no multivariable, no linear algebra. |
| 6 | **IB Math AA SL** | Lighter calculus + algebra. Below A-Level Maths. |
| 7 | **DSE Maths Compulsory + M2** | Algebra and calculus. Comparable to AP Calc AB + a slice of BC + matrices. Below A-Level Maths. |
| 8 | **AP Calc AB** | First-semester university calculus only. Limited. |
| 9 | **DSE Maths Compulsory only** | Light calculus, light algebra. Weakest of the serious-school options. |
| 10 | **IB Math AI HL/SL** | Applied / data / modelling. Inappropriate for a future physicist or pure-math student. |

**Key insight**: only **A-Level (with Further Maths)** and **AP (with post-AP additions)** offer genuine multivariable / linear-algebra exposure pre-university. IB and DSE both top out below this level.

For IGCSE: the **Cambridge Additional Math 0606** (or Edexcel 4PM1) introduces calculus at age 14–16, smoothing the jump to A-Level Maths or IB Math AA HL.

## Physics depth — calculus-based?

| Course | Calculus-based? | Approximate depth |
|---|---|---|
| **AP Physics C: Mechanics + E&M** | Yes (full) | First-year university physics for engineers (calc-based). Hardest pre-university physics in HK. |
| **A-Level Physics (CAIE 9702 / Edexcel 9PH0)** | Partial — derivations heuristic, but rigorous algebra | Comparable to IB Physics HL on breadth, more depth on key topics. Closest to AP Physics C without full calculus. |
| **IB Physics HL** | No (algebra-based by design) | Five themes, 24 topics. Broad but shallower per topic than A-Level Physics. |
| **HKDSE Physics + Elective** | No | Algebra-only. ~80% overlap with International A-Level Physics units 1–2 + selected; weaker on quantitative depth. |
| **AP Physics 1 + 2** | No | Algebra-based; conceptually tricky but mathematically lighter than A-Level/IB HL. |
| **IB Physics SL / DSE Physics (no elective)** | No | Light. Inadequate for a STEM-bound child. |

**Verdict for theoretical physics aspirations**: **AP Physics C (both halves)** is the clear leader, closely followed by **A-Level Physics + A-Level Maths**. **IB Physics HL** is acceptable only if paired with Math AA HL and supplementary work. **DSE Physics** is the weakest standalone option.

## Independent thinking / research culture — explicit emphasis

| Curriculum | Research/independent inquiry component | How structured | Real depth? |
|---|---|---|---|
| **IB DP** | Extended Essay (4,000 words) + Internal Assessments (every subject) + TOK | Built-in, mandatory | **Yes** (when school takes it seriously). The strongest structural commitment of any curriculum. |
| **AP (with Capstone)** | AP Research (4,000–5,000 words + oral defence) + AP Seminar | Two-course sequence; **only at HKIS, AIS** in HK | **Yes** at AP Capstone schools. Otherwise effectively no. |
| **AP (without Capstone)** | None mandatory | — | **No** structurally |
| **A-Level** | Optional EPQ (Extended Project Qualification, ~5,000 words) | Not always offered in HK schools | **Variable** — depends on school. Could match IB EE in depth. |
| **HKDSE** | Mostly School-Based Assessment in select subjects (lab reports, oral exams) | Light, standardized | **No** — assessments are typically formulaic. |
| **IGCSE** | None mandatory; some boards offer light coursework | — | **No** (it's a junior qualification anyway) |

**Key insight**: **IB structurally guarantees research practice.** AP Capstone matches it but is only available at ~2 HK schools. A-Level can match it via EPQ but only if the school chooses to offer EPQ. DSE structurally does not produce research thinkers.

## Verdict — which curricula best match the family's goals

### Tier 1 — Best fit (in order)

1. **A-Level (Math + Further Math + Physics + 1 more) at a school with strong EPQ + research culture**
   - Deepest pre-university STEM pathway, period.
   - Trade-off: requires a school that pairs A-Level with research practice. Without EPQ + research mentoring, A-Level can become past-paper drilling — the very thing the family wants to avoid.
   - HK candidates: Harrow, Malvern, GSIS British stream, Kellett Sixth Form. Verify each school's EPQ uptake.

2. **AP Capstone at HKIS or AIS, with Calc BC + Physics C (Mechanics + E&M)**
   - The combination of AP Research + Calc BC + Physics C produces both research practice and quantitative depth.
   - Trade-off: only ~2 schools deliver this in HK. Locks in a US-orientation.

3. **IB DP at a school that takes EE and IAs seriously, with Math AA HL + Physics HL + a STEM EE**
   - Best structural commitment to research and breadth.
   - Trade-off: math/physics depth merely good, not exceptional. Workload is the heaviest. Needs supplementary work (Olympiad, MIT OCW, etc.) to reach top STEM depth.

### Tier 2 — Hybrid strategies

- **DSE at a DSS school + supplementary APs (Calc BC, Physics C) + an external research project**
  - Preserves the **mainland JAS pathway** while adding the depth and research that DSE doesn't provide.
  - More work for the student; fewer schools deliver this combination well. Worth investigating individual DSS schools (`02-schools/`).

- **A-Level + 1–2 APs (e.g., AP Calc BC, AP Physics C as enhancers)**
  - For an A-Level student wanting US options — adds AP credit/placement and US-friendly currency.

### Tier 3 — Pure DSE / pure AP (without Capstone)

- **Pure DSE**: justifiable only if the mainland pathway is dominant in the family's planning *and* the school is exceptional. Otherwise, exam culture risk too high.
- **Pure AP without Capstone**: little structural inquiry; depends entirely on student initiative.

### Critical caveats

- **The school matters more than the curriculum.** A great teacher in a DSE school can outperform a mediocre teacher in IB. The school file (`02-schools/`) should be the next axis.
- **The math choice within each curriculum matters more than the curriculum label.** IB Math AA HL > IB Math AI HL by a wide margin. A-Level + Further Maths > A-Level Maths alone. AP Calc BC > AP Calc AB. DSE M2 ≫ DSE without extension.
- **Research practice can be added externally.** Olympiads, MIT OCW, summer programmes (RSI, Yau Math Camp, BWSI) work alongside any curriculum and may be the most important single thing the family invests in.
- **The mainland JAS option is irreversibly lost** if the child does not take HKDSE. This is a one-way door. For a family with strong mainland China ties, this deserves explicit consideration.

### One-line recommendations

- **For maximum STEM depth + flexibility**: A-Level with Further Maths at Harrow / Malvern HK + EPQ.
- **For maximum research culture + breadth**: IB DP at a strong IB school (CIS, GSIS, etc.) with Math AA HL + Physics HL + STEM-focused EE.
- **For US-pipeline + STEM depth + research**: AP Capstone at HKIS or AIS with Calc BC + Physics C (Mech + E&M).
- **If mainland universities are a top priority**: DSE at a strong DSS school with M2 + supplementary AP/research.
