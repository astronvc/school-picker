# IB — International Baccalaureate (PYP, MYP, DP)

## TL;DR for this family

- **Strong fit** for "independent thinking + research" goal: TOK, Extended Essay, IAs (internal assessments) all force original investigation. This is the single most distinctive feature of the IB.
- **Math is a real concern**: Math AA HL is genuinely strong (proof-based, calculus, theory) and is the right choice for a STEM-leaning child. Math AI is applied/data-focused and not appropriate for a future physics/math student. Math AA SL is a clear step down from A-Level Maths.
- **Physics HL is solid but not deep enough for a budding theoretical physicist** — comparable in depth to AP Physics 1+2, weaker than AP Physics C or A-Level Physics for calculus-based mechanics/E&M.
- **Workload is the main downside** — six subjects + TOK + EE + CAS + IAs runs ~35–45 hrs/week including school. Burnout is a documented, common outcome.
- **Universally recognized** in HK, US, UK, mainland China (Tsinghua/Peking accept). Best universal passport of any single curriculum.

## Programme structure

The IB runs three age-group programmes; **a school may offer one, two, or all three**. Most HK international schools offer DP at minimum; many also run MYP and PYP.

| Programme | Ages | Years | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| PYP (Primary Years) | 3–12 | K–G6 | Inquiry-based, transdisciplinary; no exams. |
| MYP (Middle Years) | 11–16 | G6–G10 | 8 subject groups + Personal Project (G10). eAssessments optional. Often criticised as light vs IGCSE. |
| DP (Diploma) | 16–19 | G11–G12 | The hard programme. 6 subjects + 3 core (TOK, EE, CAS). 2-year cycle. |
| CP (Career-related) | 16–19 | G11–G12 | Vocational. Rare in HK. |

This file is mostly about the **DP**, which is what determines university outcomes.

## DP structure

**Six subjects, one from each of five groups + a sixth from any group:**

| Group | Examples | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1: Studies in Language and Literature | English A: Lang/Lit, Chinese A: Lang/Lit | "A" = first language. |
| 2: Language Acquisition | English B, French B, Spanish ab initio | "B" = second language. |
| 3: Individuals and Societies | History, Economics, Geography, Psychology, Business Mgmt, Global Politics | |
| 4: Sciences | Physics, Chemistry, Biology, Computer Science, Design Tech, Environmental Systems | |
| 5: Mathematics | Math AA, Math AI | Choose one + level. |
| 6: The Arts | Visual Arts, Music, Theatre, Film | OR a second subject from groups 2–4. |

Each subject is taken at **HL (Higher Level, ~240 hours)** or **SL (Standard Level, ~150 hours)**. Students take **3 HL + 3 SL** (or 4 HL + 2 SL, allowed but heavy).

**Each subject scored 1–7** (max 42). Plus **3 bonus points** from the TOK/EE matrix. **Max score = 45**. Diploma awarded with ≥24 points (and various failing conditions). Bilingual Diploma is awarded if a student takes two language A's.

### The Core (mandatory, not subjects)

| Component | What it is | Hours | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| TOK (Theory of Knowledge) | Epistemology course. 1,600-word essay (external) + exhibition (internal, 950 words). | ~100h | Forces students to argue about how knowledge is constructed across disciplines. Quality varies wildly by teacher. |
| EE (Extended Essay) | 4,000-word independent research paper in a chosen subject. | ~40h supervised | This is the closest analogue to a university dissertation. Major signal to admissions. |
| CAS (Creativity, Activity, Service) | 18 months of structured extracurriculars + reflection. | ~150h | Pass/fail. Often a checkbox exercise but can be substantive. |

TOK + EE produces a 0–3 bonus point matrix. Failing either forfeits the diploma.

### Recent syllabus changes 2024–2026

- **Physics, Chemistry, Biology** — new guides first taught Aug 2023, **first exams May 2025**. Optional Paper 3 topics removed; content folded into 24 mandatory topics across 5 themes. One fewer paper. Internal assessment is now an open-ended Scientific Investigation.
- **Mathematics AA / AI** — current guides since 2019/first exams 2021; **stable through 2026**. Next review pending; no announced first-teaching date as of May 2026.
- **Computer Science** — new guide first teaching Aug 2025, first exams May 2027. Case study now in Paper 1; Paper 3 dropped.
- **History, Design Tech, Economics, Marine Science** — refreshed 2024–2026 (sustainability, digital economy emphasized).
- **Languages, Theatre, Music** — incremental updates only.

Source: ibo.org curriculum updates page (see Sources).

## Math: AA vs AI, HL vs SL

**This is the single most important DP decision for a STEM child.**

| Course | Style | Calculus | Proof | Use case |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Math AA HL** | Pure & applied; proof-led | Single-variable calculus, complex numbers, vectors, series | Yes — induction, deductive proofs throughout | Intended pathway for math, physics, engineering. Equivalent in depth to A-Level Maths + ~half of Further Maths content, depending on optional topic. |
| Math AA SL | Pure-leaning, lighter | Differentiation + integration, simpler | Some | Adequate for econ, social science. Weak for engineering admission; many top STEM programmes prefer HL. |
| Math AI HL | Applied; statistics & modelling | Yes but lighter; more numerical | Limited | Designed for econ, business, social science. NOT a path to physics/engineering. |
| Math AI SL | Applied, very light | Minimal | None | Quantitative literacy. Avoid for any STEM trajectory. |

**Verdict for this family: Math AA HL is the only correct choice if the child is keeping options open for math/physics/engineering.** AA HL is genuinely rigorous and proof-aware; not as deep as Cambridge Further Maths but considerably more conceptually demanding than DSE Math + M2 in proof culture.

Approx 14.6% of Math AA HL candidates achieved a 7 in May 2024 — making it one of the higher 7-rate HL subjects, partly because self-selection into AA HL skews toward strong students. Don't read this as "easy."

## Physics HL

**Five themes, 24 topics, two papers + Scientific Investigation IA. First exams 2025.**

Themes: A. Space, time and motion · B. Particulate nature of matter · C. Wave behaviour · D. Fields · E. Nuclear and quantum physics.

**Honest assessment:**
- Calculus is *used* but the syllabus is calculus-light by design (the IB does not require Math HL as a co-requisite). Many proofs and derivations are heuristic rather than rigorous.
- Compared to **AP Physics C** (calculus-based mechanics + E&M, first-year college equivalent), IB Physics HL is **broader but shallower**.
- Compared to **A-Level Physics**, IB Physics HL is comparable in difficulty but A-Level rewards more depth on fewer topics.
- For a child who will study theoretical physics: IB Physics HL + Math AA HL is acceptable but not optimal. Self-study or external math (e.g., AMC, Olympiad, MIT OCW) is essential to reach competitive depth.

## Internal Assessments (IAs)

Every DP subject has an internally-assessed component — typically 20–30% of the grade. For Math AA HL it is a 12–20 page **Mathematical Exploration** on a topic of the student's choosing. For Physics HL it is a **Scientific Investigation**. For History it is a **Historical Investigation**. These force original research; collectively they take ~150–250 hours over the two years.

This is the part of the IB that delivers on the "independent research" promise — when teachers take it seriously. In schools with weak IA coaching, students Wikipedia-mine and the experience is hollow.

## University recognition

### Hong Kong
- **HKU, CUHK, HKUST, PolyU, CityU, BU, EdUHK, LingnanU**: All accept IB DP via **Non-JUPAS / International** admissions schemes (not via JUPAS — JUPAS is HKDSE-only).
- Typical thresholds: HKU 30+, CUHK 30+, HKUST 28+. Medicine/Dentistry/Law/Quant Finance: 40+ with specific HL subject conditions.
- IB-Match guide and HKU/CUHK admissions pages confirm IB is among the most commonly recognized international qualifications.

### United Kingdom
- All UK universities accept IB. Cambridge typical conditional offer: **40–42 with 776 at HL** (depends on course). Oxford similar. UCL ~36–38.
- The 5**(HKDSE)/A*(A-Level)/7(IB HL) equivalency is well-established, though IB HL 7 is statistically rarer than A* at A-Level.

### United States
- IB is well-known and well-regarded. AP/IB/A-Level treated as different but equivalent rigor signals.
- Princeton, Columbia, Cornell, UPenn award some credit/placement for HL 6/7. Harvard, Yale, MIT generally do not award credit for IB but will use it for placement.
- The **EE is a tangible writing sample** that admissions officers see; this is an underrated advantage in US holistic admissions.

### Mainland China
- Tsinghua and Peking accept IB for international applicants. Tsinghua has no fixed IB cutoff, but competitive applicants score 38+ with strong HL subjects (often 7,7,6).
- For a HK-passport-holder, the **Joint Admission Scheme** (HKDSE-only) is *not* an option — IB students apply via international tracks. This is a meaningful trade-off (see DSE.md).
- Some mainland universities (e.g., Tsinghua's Schwarzman, NYU Shanghai, Duke Kunshan) are highly receptive to IB.

## Workload and weaknesses (be honest)

**Strengths:**
- Forces original research (EE, IAs) better than any other mainstream curriculum.
- Genuinely interdisciplinary; TOK can be transformative if taught well.
- Universal recognition — a single passport to all four geographies of interest.
- Math AA HL + Physics HL is a respectable STEM combination (with caveats above).

**Weaknesses:**
- **Workload**: 6 subjects + 3 core components + ~6 IAs simultaneously. Most students sleep <6 hours/night during the IA crunch (Q1–Q2 of Year 13). Burnout, anxiety widely reported.
- **Cannot specialize**: a future mathematician must still take a Group 6 art or a second humanity, a language B, etc. A-Level allows 4 STEM subjects; IB does not.
- **Math depth**: AA HL ≠ A-Level Further Maths in proof rigor or topic depth (no group theory, less mechanics, less complex analysis).
- **Physics calculus rigor**: weaker than AP Physics C / A-Level Physics for a quantitative student.
- **Quality variance**: TOK and EE quality depend heavily on the teacher/supervisor. A bad supervisor can sink the EE.
- **Cost**: typically taught only in private/international schools; per-exam fees are higher than DSE/A-Level.

## Bottom line for this family

IB DP **strongly aligns with the philosophy** (independent thinking, research, breadth, anti-rote). The EE and IAs are exactly what 做题/填鸭式 is *not*.

The **caveat is math/physics depth**: a child aiming at theoretical physics or competition math will need to supplement IB with Olympiad training, AMC/AIME, or MIT/Tsinghua online coursework. With Math AA HL + Physics HL + a serious EE in physics or math, IB is a credible STEM pathway — just not the deepest one.

For a child who would go to mainland top universities (清北), the absence of the Joint Admission Scheme route is a real cost. IB students go via international tracks where competition is fiercer per seat.
