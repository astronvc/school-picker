# Mainland-university paths for a HK passport holder

A common confusion — there are **three different routes** for a HK passport child to apply to mainland Chinese universities. They are not interchangeable.

## Path 1 — Joint Admission Scheme (JAS) via HKDSE

**Formal name**: 内地高校招收香港中学文凭考试学生计划 ("Mainland universities admitting HKDSE candidates").

- **Eligibility**: HK PR / passport holders who sit HKDSE.
- **Mechanism**: 165 mainland universities (2026/27 cycle, including Tsinghua, Peking, Fudan, Zhejiang) accept HKDSE results directly. **No mainland exam required.** Application via JAS portal alongside JUPAS.
- **Quota**: Reserved seats — quota-protected for HK students. By far the largest, most welcoming track to mainland top universities for HK kids.
- **Curriculum requirement**: child must take HKDSE.

This is the path referenced by the curriculum comparison and key-insights files when they say "the mainland JAS pathway is the strongest single argument for DSE." It's the easiest mainland-university route — but only if the child sits DSE.

Source: [GovHK 2026/27 announcement](https://www.info.gov.hk/gia/general/202512/01/P2025120100297.htm)

## Path 2 — HMT/Huaqiao Joint Examination (港澳台华侨联招)

**Formal name**: 华侨港澳台学生联合招生考试 ("Joint Admission Examination for Hong Kong, Macao, Taiwan and Overseas Chinese students").

- **Eligibility**: HK / Macao / Taiwan PR holders with Chinese nationality, plus overseas Chinese with foreign PR. The user's child as HK passport holder + Chinese nationality background likely qualifies (verify residency requirements).
- **Mechanism**: separate exam, taken in mainland China; covers Chinese, English, Math + 3 electives (sciences or humanities track). Easier than Gaokao but still a real exam.
- **Quota**: smaller and more competitive than JAS (Path 1). Acceptance thresholds vary by university.
- **Curriculum requirement**: doesn't require DSE — but the exam is in Chinese and tests mainland-style knowledge. Most candidates who take it have done significant prep.

This is what the user asked about ("华侨联考是吗?"). It exists as a fallback but is not the same as the DSE-based JAS path.

## Path 3 — International undergraduate admissions (IB / AP / A-Level + supporting docs)

**Formal name**: varies by university — typically 国际本科招生 or "International Undergraduate Programme".

- **Eligibility**: any student with a recognized international qualification (IB DP, AP, A-Level) plus often SAT/ACT/TOEFL.
- **Mechanism**: each mainland top university has its own international undergraduate admissions track (e.g. Tsinghua International, Peking University HSBC Business School). Applications are direct to the university.
- **Quota**: small relative to JAS or Gaokao; competitive. Tsinghua / Peking / Fudan are looking for IB 38+ with strong HL or A*A*A in three relevant subjects, plus SAT 1500+ / TOEFL 110+.
- **Curriculum requirement**: any of IB / AP / A-Level. Most flexible.

This route is **viable but not a replacement** for the JAS path's scale and quota. It's how non-DSE international-school students apply to Tsinghua / Peking — and how, if mainland is "optional" not primary, the family can keep the door cracked open even if the child does IB or A-Level.

## Implication for the family

Since mainland top universities are "optional" rather than primary:

- **No need to bias curriculum toward DSE for this reason.**
- IB DP, A-Level, AP all preserve a credible (smaller, more competitive) path via Path 3.
- If mainland becomes more important later (e.g. by Y10–11 the family decides 清北 should be primary), the child can either:
  - Pivot to an HMT 港澳台联招 prep year (Path 2), OR
  - Apply via international undergraduate admissions (Path 3) with strong IB/A-Level results.
- The "one-way door" caveat from the comparison-matrix only fully applies if the family wants to maximise mainland-university optionality. Given mainland is optional, choose curriculum based on the family's other priorities (independent thinking, math/physics depth, future skills) — which point to **IB or A-Level**, not DSE.
