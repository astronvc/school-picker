# Hong Kong International Schools (Non-ESF)

This file covers the major international schools in Hong Kong that operate independently of the English Schools Foundation. ESF schools have their own file (`esf-schools.md`). DSS (semi-government) schools and PIS (Private Independent Schools that are also DSS-like) live in `dss-schools.md` and `private-independent.md`.

The HK international landscape is dominated by ~50 EDB-listed international schools serving ~40,000 students. Annual tuition for the top tier now runs HK$200,000–290,000, and most also require either a debenture (HK$3M–20M, partially refundable / depreciating) or an annual capital levy (HK$25K–60K). The "no rote learning" family will likely find the IB Continuum schools (CIS, ISF, CDNIS, ESF, RCHK, DC, HKA, Carmel, Malvern, Stamford) most aligned with their values; HKIS (US/AP) and Harrow (UK A-Level) are more traditional but very strong on outcomes.

## Detailed school profiles

### Chinese International School (CIS)
- **Founded:** 1983 in Mid-Levels by HK families seeking dual-language education; campus on Braemar Hill (Eastern District) plus a Hangzhou residential campus opened 2018.
- **Curriculum:** IB Middle Years Programme (Y7–9), school-developed bridge Y10–11, IB Diploma Y12–13. Defining characteristic: **dual-language English + Mandarin from Reception**, with subjects taught in both languages — not just language classes.
- **Tuition (2025/26):** Reception HK$167,700; Y1–6 HK$222,300; Y7–11 HK$262,500; Y12–13 HK$266,100 (Hangzhou Y10 boarding extra).
- **Debenture / Nomination:** Corporate Nomination Right (CNR) **HK$15M for 2026 entry**; non-refundable, depreciates to zero over 5 years. Family debentures from secondary market. Without nomination, admission is highly competitive (siblings and alumni get priority).
- **2025 IB results:** **Avg 38.95** (out of 45), 99% pass, 49% scored 40+. (2024 was 39.7 with 63% over 40.)
- **University destinations:** ~50% USA, ~30% UK, ~10% Canada, ~5% HK, plus AU/Europe/Asia. Strong Ivy + Oxbridge pipeline.
- **Philosophy:** Inquiry-based via IB MYP/DP; inquiry but academically demanding. The Hangzhou Year 10 is unique in HK and signals genuine commitment to mainland Chinese cultural exposure.
- **Math / science depth:** Strong but not specifically known as a "STEM" school; academic profile is broad.
- **Mandarin:** **Strongest of the major IB internationals** alongside ISF, YCIS and VSA. Dual-language is built into the model rather than bolted-on.
- **Mainland-background family fit:** **Excellent.** Many CIS families are HKID-holders with mainland heritage. Mandarin is celebrated; the Hangzhou trip is a genuine immersion.
- **Honest weakness:** Eye-watering nomination economics. Without a CNR, getting in is hard. Some criticism that academic intensity can creep up.

### Hong Kong International School (HKIS)
- **Founded:** 1966 by the Lutheran Church-Missouri Synod and a group of US business families. Two campuses: Tai Tam (Upper Primary–HS) and Repulse Bay (Reception–G4).
- **Curriculum:** US standards-based (proprietary curriculum); 29 AP subjects in High School (29 currently offered).
- **Tuition (2025/26):** HK$247,800–286,300 across grades.
- **Debenture:** Family / Corporate Debentures HK$3M–5M, must be bought direct from school. **No secondary market** — strict no-resale policy. Annual Capital Levy ~HK$24,500.
- **Outcomes:** No IB; ~80% of seniors gain admission to top US universities. Heavy Ivy + UC + NESCAC pipeline.
- **Philosophy:** Christian (Lutheran) ethos but not evangelical. Progressive American liberal-arts approach with strong arts and athletics. Less rote than HK locals; more traditional than IB schools in structure.
- **Math / science depth:** Excellent AP STEM portfolio (Calc BC, Physics C, CS-A, Stats, Chem, Bio).
- **Mandarin:** Required Reception–G5; optional G6–12 with two streams (Near-Native + Mandarin-as-Second). Not a bilingual school.
- **Mainland-background family fit:** Possible but the school's American Christian identity and US-focused outcome means PRC-heritage families often choose CIS / CDNIS / ISF instead.
- **Honest weakness:** Christian identity may not suit all; debenture cost is steep with no resale value.

### International Christian School (ICS)
- **Founded:** 1992; campus in Shek Mun, Sha Tin.
- **Curriculum:** US college-prep curriculum + 21 AP courses in HS. WASC accredited.
- **Tuition (2026/27):** HK$96,400–212,500.
- **Debenture:** HK$250K for first child; family discount from third child.
- **Outcomes (2023–24):** 511 AP exams, 87% scored 3+, 62% scored 4 or 5.
- **Philosophy:** Evangelical Christian — explicit Biblical worldview integrated through curriculum (more than HKIS). Statement of faith required of staff.
- **Math / science depth:** Solid AP STEM pipeline.
- **Mandarin:** Mandarin required; not bilingual.
- **Mainland-background family fit:** Religious identity is a defining feature; non-Christian families should think carefully about whether the integrated faith approach works for them.

### Australian International School Hong Kong (AISHK)
- **Founded:** 1995; campus in Kowloon Tong.
- **Curriculum:** Australian (NSW HSC) early years to Year 12; from Year 11 students choose **HSC or IB Diploma**. Sept–June calendar (uniquely Australian alignment).
- **Tuition (2026/27):** HK$91,000–201,500; first-year enrolment ~HK$277,700 with one-time fees. 10% discount from third sibling.
- **Outcomes:** Solid HSC + IB results; many graduates to Group of 8 Australian universities and increasingly UK/US.
- **Philosophy:** Inquiry-based, holistic — Australian model is less exam-heavy than UK/HK.
- **Math / science depth:** Standard Australian + IB rigor.
- **Mandarin:** Available; not bilingual.
- **Mainland-background family fit:** Welcoming. Australian path is a credible "alt route" if Australia is potential university destination.

### Canadian International School of Hong Kong (CDNIS)
- **Founded:** 1991; campus in Aberdeen (South Side).
- **Curriculum:** Full IB Continuum (PYP, MYP, DP) + Ontario Secondary School Diploma.
- **Tuition (2025/26):** Up to HK$290,800 first year inc. fees; HK$80K reservation deposit.
- **2025 IB:** Avg 37.7; 38% over 40; 4 students at 44, 4 at 43.
- **Philosophy:** Inquiry- and project-based via IB. Strong Chinese language emphasis (bilingual track from EY1) — a notable USP among the western-tradition schools.
- **Math / science depth:** Solid IB outcomes; not a STEM specialist but strong.
- **Mandarin:** Bilingual English + Mandarin track from Early Years; about 40% of students are of Chinese heritage.
- **Mainland-background family fit:** Very strong — one of the favourite "international" choices for HK-passport / mainland-heritage families.
- **Honest weakness:** Intake competitive; long waitlists at primary.

### German Swiss International School (GSIS)
- **Founded:** 1969; Peak campus.
- **Curriculum:** Two streams under one roof:
  - **GIS (German International Stream)** → German International Abitur (DIA)
  - **EIS (English International Stream)** → IGCSE + IB Diploma
  Multilingual: German, English, Mandarin (from Y2), Latin, French.
- **Tuition (2025/26):** HK$161,450–203,420 (no annual levy).
- **Debenture:** HK$6M Infrastructure Debenture grants "First Admissions Priority"; first-year cost ~HK$711,700.
- **2025 IB:** **Avg 41.0** — among HK's top, with 75% scoring 40+ and 100% pass rate.
- **University destinations:** Strong Europe (esp. Germany), HK, UK, USA.
- **Philosophy:** Academic rigor; explicit "German excellence" identity. Not progressive in the IB-PYP sense — more structured, more language-focused, more demanding.
- **Math / science depth:** Excellent. German Abitur is famously rigorous on math/physics; the EIS IB results suggest strong STEM throughout.
- **Mandarin:** From Year 2; trilingual (Eng-Ger-Man) is the goal.
- **Mainland-background family fit:** Acceptable; the EIS stream doesn't require German fluency. Heritage/community feels European but the IB stream is internationally diverse.
- **Honest weakness:** German-Swiss cultural ethos can feel formal; competitive entry.

### Stamford American School Hong Kong (SAIS)
- **Founded:** 2017 (part of Cognita group via Singapore Stamford); Ho Man Tin primary, new West Kowloon Pre-University campus opening 2026.
- **Curriculum:** American (AERO + Common Core Plus) + IB DP option; **STEMinn** programme from age 5. Three diploma options including IB.
- **Tuition (2025/26):** HK$217,100–264,000.
- **Capital Levy:** HK$150K one-off OR HK$30K/yr.
- **Outcomes:** Newer school; first IB cohorts emerging.
- **Philosophy:** Inquiry + STEM-forward; Cognita backing.
- **Mandarin:** Daily mandatory Mandarin K–G5.
- **Mainland-background family fit:** Welcoming; relatively easy admissions (newer school, capacity exists).
- **Honest note:** Less established track record than legacy schools. New WKL HS campus is a real plus for HS years.

### Harrow International School Hong Kong
- **Founded:** 2012 (HK first international franchise of Harrow School UK, founded 1572). Campus in Tuen Mun.
- **Curriculum:** EYFS → English National Curriculum → IGCSE Y11 → A-Level Y12–13 with optional EPQ.
- **Tuition (2025/26):** HK$166,671–197,930 + ACL HK$60,000.
- **Debenture:** Substantial (HK$11M+) for priority.
- **Outcomes:** Strong A-Level results, regular Oxbridge / Ivy / top-30 destinations.
- **Philosophy:** Traditional British boarding-school ethos: houses, leadership, "values". More structured than IB schools. Not as project-based.
- **Math / science depth:** A-Level Maths/Further Maths/Physics is famously strong if students opt in. STEM Olympiad culture.
- **Mandarin:** Putonghua taught; not bilingual.
- **Mainland-background family fit:** Mainland-heritage families well represented (Harrow brand has huge prestige in PRC). Boarding option attractive.
- **Honest weakness:** Tuen Mun is far from Central / South Side; very competitive.

### Malvern College Hong Kong
- **Founded:** 2018 (UK Malvern brand, founded 1865). Tai Po campus.
- **Curriculum:** IB Continuum (PYP, MYP, DP) — distinct from Malvern UK's A-Level model.
- **Tuition (2025/26):** HK$209,429–268,210.
- **2025 IB:** Avg 39.0; 33% over 40.
- **Philosophy:** UK boarding-school heritage but IB delivery — combines British structure with IB inquiry.
- **Mandarin:** Strong programme.
- **Mainland-background family fit:** Newer school with capacity; brand resonates with PRC families.

### Yew Chung International School (YCIS)
- **Founded:** 1932 (oldest HK-founded international school). Campuses in Kowloon Tong and Tseung Kwan O.
- **Curriculum:** YCIS school-developed PYP equivalent + IGCSE + IB DP.
- **Tuition (2025/26):** HK$146,355–272,140.
- **Defining feature:** **Co-teaching model** — every classroom has BOTH a native English-speaking and a native Chinese-speaking lead teacher who plan and deliver together. Genuine immersion, not just language classes.
- **Mandarin:** Strongest co-teaching bilingual model in HK.
- **Mainland-background family fit:** Excellent; bilingualism is the brand.
- **Honest weakness:** Less academic prestige than CIS or top ESF; stronger as "values + bilingual" play than "Ivy pipeline".

### Singapore International School (Hong Kong) (SIS)
- **Founded:** 1991. Aberdeen (Nam Long Shan).
- **Curriculum:** Singapore primary curriculum (P1–P6) → secondary → IGCSE → IB DP.
- **Tuition (2025/26):** HK$123,000–274,900. Singapore-citizen subsidy HK$16,100/yr.
- **2025 IB:** **Avg 39.0; 47% scored 40+.**
- **Philosophy:** Singapore primary is famously rigorous on math (Singapore Math) and science. Bilingual English + Mandarin built in.
- **Math / science depth:** **Among the best in HK for math/science fundamentals.** This stands out for the family's brief.
- **Mandarin:** Bilingual English + Mandarin from primary.
- **Mainland-background family fit:** Excellent. Many ethnic Chinese, Singapore-passport, HKID-PRC-heritage students.
- **Honest weakness:** Singapore math style is rigorous and structured — not the "no exams, no problem-sets" model. But the rigor is conceptual, not pure rote, and exactly what builds STEM fundamentals.

### French International School (FIS / LFI)
- **Founded:** 1963. Multi-campus: Happy Valley (Intl Stream), Jardine's Lookout, Chai Wan (French stream), Tseung Kwan O.
- **Curriculum:**
  - **French Stream:** French national curriculum → Brevet → Bac (incl. BFI / BFI in English).
  - **International Stream:** IPC primary → IMYC → IGCSE → IB DP.
- **Tuition:** ~HK$160,000–240,000+ depending on stream and year.
- **Mandarin:** Available, especially in International Stream.
- **Mainland-background family fit:** Less mainland-focused; community is Francophone-leaning. International Stream is more inclusive.

### Korean International School (KIS)
- **Founded:** 1988. Sai Wan Ho.
- **Curriculum:** Two sections — International (British Cambridge / IPC / BTEC) and Korean.
- **Tuition (2025/26):** HK$93,400–131,700 (among the most affordable).
- **Mandarin:** Available, not bilingual.
- **Mainland-background family fit:** Possible, but cultural lean is Korean.

### Japanese International School (JIS)
- **Founded:** 1985. Tai Po.
- **Curriculum:** Japanese curric. + English-medium IB MYP/DP stream.
- **Tuition (2025/26):** HK$156,420–264,000.
- **Mainland-background family fit:** Niche; primarily expat Japanese.

### Hong Kong Academy (HKA)
- **Founded:** 2000. Sai Kung (Sai Sha).
- **Curriculum:** IB Continuum (PYP, MYP, DP).
- **Tuition (2025/26):** HK$116,570–264,700; **Family Debenture HK$630,000** OR Annual Capital Levy HK$32K.
- **Philosophy:** Most explicitly progressive of the major IB schools. Inclusion of learning differences; small classes; outdoor learning; strong arts. Not high-stakes.
- **Math / science depth:** IB-standard; not a STEM specialist — academically rigorous within IB norms but less competitive culture.
- **Mandarin:** Optional Mandarin; not bilingual.
- **Mainland-background family fit:** Mixed; HKA's progressive ethos and strong learning-support culture appeals to families wanting low-pressure environment but may not feel "academic" enough for math/physics-focused mainland-background parents.

### Nord Anglia International School Hong Kong (NAIS HK)
- **Founded:** 2014. Lam Tin (primary) + Kwun Tong (secondary).
- **Curriculum:** Cambridge IGCSE → IB DP (dual-track senior school).
- **Tuition (2025/26):** HK$91,300–222,500. **New Annual Capital Levy HK$35K from 2026/27** replaces previous CEF.
- **2025 IB:** Avg 35.4; 31% over 40.
- **Philosophy:** Modern, tech-forward. **MIT partnership** for STEAM curriculum; Juilliard for performing arts. Global Nord Anglia network with student exchanges.
- **Math / science depth:** STEAM-MIT branding is real but academic outcomes are middle-of-pack relative to GSIS/CIS.
- **Mainland-background family fit:** Welcoming; Nord Anglia brand strong in PRC.

### ESF Renaissance College (RCHK)
- See `esf-schools.md` for fuller treatment.
- **Curriculum:** All four IB programmes (PYP, MYP, DP, CP) — rare globally.
- **Tuition (2025/26):** HK$148,100–195,700; one-off NCL HK$50K.
- **2025 IB:** Avg 36.0.
- **Mandarin:** Standard ESF programme.

### Carmel School (Elsa High School)
- **Founded:** 1992 (Jewish community school). Three campuses: Robinson Road (ELC), Mid-Levels Borrett Road (Elementary), Shau Kei Wan (Elsa HS).
- **Curriculum:** IB Continuum.
- **Tuition (2025/26):** HK$80,500–226,090.
- **2025 IB:** Avg 38.1; 43% over 40.
- **Philosophy:** Jewish ethos but accepts non-Jewish students (~30% of intake at Elsa High). Small, warm community.
- **Mandarin:** Mandatory from kindergarten.
- **Mainland-background family fit:** Possible; small community is welcoming but the Jewish identity is genuinely lived and may not be the obvious fit.

### Victoria Shanghai Academy (VSA)
- **Founded:** 2004 (under the Victoria Educational Organisation). Shum Wan, Aberdeen.
- **Curriculum:** IB Continuum, **bilingual English + Putonghua primary**, secondary in English with strong Chinese support.
- **Tuition:** ~HK$130,000–230,000 (verify on school site).
- **2025 IB:** Avg 37.6, **5 perfect scorers**.
- **Mandarin:** Strongly bilingual; among the best for HK families wanting authentic Mandarin.
- **Mainland-background family fit:** **Excellent.** Many HK-passport / mainland-heritage families. Often ranked alongside CIS/ISF for best bilingual fit.

## Heritage / brand cluster summary

- **Top "old guard" + bilingual:** CIS, ESF (KGV, Island), HKIS, GSIS — long-standing, strong IB/AP outcomes.
- **UK boarding-school franchises:** Harrow HK (2012), Shrewsbury HK (2018), Malvern HK (2018), Mount Kelly HK — see `private-independent.md`.
- **Bilingual-immersion specialists:** CIS, ISF, YCIS, VSA, CDNIS, SIS — true Mandarin pathways.
- **Chain / for-profit network:** Nord Anglia HK, Stamford American HK, Cognita schools.
- **Community / faith-based:** ICS (Christian evangelical), Carmel (Jewish), HKIS (Lutheran heritage), French (Francophone).
- **Progressive / inclusive:** HKA, RCHK, Discovery College.
