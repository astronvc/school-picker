# Private Independent Schools — UK Boarding-Brand Wave + Other PIS

This file covers schools that are **neither ESF nor part of the Direct Subsidy Scheme**. They run on a fully fee-paying / debenture model. Most fit one of two profiles:

1. **UK boarding-school franchises** — Harrow, Shrewsbury, Malvern, Mount Kelly. They licence the brand from a famous British boarding school and run a HK day-school version of it (with optional boarding). This wave began with **Harrow HK in 2012** and accelerated 2018–2024.
2. **Established for-profit / chain operators** — Nord Anglia (UK group), Stamford American (Cognita / SG group), Kellett (UK-style), German Swiss (already covered as Intl).

Some schools that look "private independent" are actually licensed under EDB's **Private Independent School (PIS) Scheme**, where the government grants land/initial capital but the school operates independently with its own curriculum (e.g. Kellett, ICHK, ISF Academy).

For the family's brief, this category matters because:
- Some of these schools (Harrow, Malvern, Shrewsbury) have **strong UK A-Level or IB STEM** track records.
- Most are **welcoming to mainland-heritage HK families** because PRC parents recognise UK boarding-school brands.
- Fees are at the **top of the market** (HK$170K–270K + capital levy HK$30K–60K + debenture HK$3M–20M).

## Detailed school profiles

### Harrow International School Hong Kong
- **Founded:** 2012 — Harrow's first international franchise after Bangkok and Beijing.
- **Campus:** Tuen Mun (large, purpose-built campus + boarding houses).
- **Curriculum:** English National Curriculum from Reception; **GCSEs in Y11; A-Levels + EPQ in Sixth Form**. No IB.
- **Tuition (2025/26):** HK$166,671 (Pre-Prep) – HK$197,930 (Sixth Form).
- **Capital Levy:** **HK$60,000/yr** (highest among HK internationals).
- **Debenture:** Debentures HK$11M+ for top-priority admissions.
- **Outcomes:** Strong A-Level results; consistent Oxbridge / Imperial / LSE / Ivy pipeline.
- **Boarding:** Yes — full boarding houses with Harrow's traditional "House" system.
- **Philosophy:** Traditional British boarding values: leadership, sport, "mind-body-soul", chapel, houses. Structured, formal, character-driven.
- **Math/Science depth:** **Excellent** for students opting into Maths / Further Maths / Physics A-Level. Strong STEM Olympiad presence.
- **Mandarin:** Putonghua taught; not bilingual.
- **Mainland-background family fit:** **Very strong.** Harrow brand is gold-standard among PRC families. Boarding option attractive for families travelling between HK and mainland.
- **Honest weakness:** Tuen Mun is far from Central / South Side / Mid-Levels (1+ hour commute). Highly traditional culture is not for everyone. Capital costs are top of market.

### Shrewsbury International School Hong Kong
- **Founded:** 2018 (Primary). **Year 7 launching 2026/27** with secondary build-out planned through to A-Level.
- **Campus:** Tseung Kwan O.
- **Curriculum:** English National Curriculum primary → IGCSE → A-Level (rolling out as cohorts age up).
- **Tuition Y7 (2026/27):** HK$235,080.
- **Capital Levy:** Annual Capital Levy OR Capital Certificate (valid 4 years; exempts holder from ACL).
- **Outcomes:** Primary cohort still maturing; UK Shrewsbury academic heritage gives strong brand.
- **Philosophy:** Shrewsbury's traditional UK pre-Sixth-Form approach plus international perspective.
- **Math/Science depth:** UK Shrewsbury is well-known for Maths and Sciences; A-Level cohort still emerging.
- **Mandarin:** Available.
- **Mainland-background family fit:** Welcoming; brand-friendly.
- **Honest note:** **Newest of the UK brands**, secondary cohort still building. Suitable if families can take the long view.

### Malvern College Hong Kong
- **Founded:** 2018; campus in Tai Po.
- **Curriculum:** IB Continuum (PYP, MYP, DP) — distinct from UK Malvern's A-Level model. (Reflects HK preference for IB international portability.)
- **Tuition (2025/26):** HK$209,429–268,210.
- **2025 IB:** Avg 39.0 (33% over 40).
- **Philosophy:** UK Malvern's "Be Bold" + IB inquiry. Co-ed, modern facilities.
- **Math/Science depth:** Solid IB STEM.
- **Mandarin:** Strong programme.
- **Mainland-background family fit:** Newer school with capacity; PRC-family friendly.

### Mount Kelly School Hong Kong
- **Founded:** 2017 (Pre-Prep + Prep). Tsim Sha Tsui.
- **Curriculum:** EYFS + English National Curriculum to ~Y9. **Currently Prep school only** (Y1–9 build out; no GCSE / A-Level yet on this campus).
- **Tuition (2025/26):** Y1–2 HK$154,990 → Y9 HK$195,000.
- **Capital Levy:** Modest.
- **Outcomes:** Prep school — students transition to senior schools elsewhere (often UK boarding via Mount Kelly UK referral, or other HK schools).
- **Philosophy:** Traditional UK prep; small-class sizes; daily Putonghua + French from Y1.
- **Math/Science depth:** UK prep emphasis on numeracy fundamentals.
- **Mandarin:** Daily Putonghua.
- **Mainland-background family fit:** Welcoming; small enough to be intimate.
- **Honest note:** **No through-train** — families must plan for senior-school transition (often back to UK or to other HK secondaries).

### Kellett School
- **Founded:** 1976 by a group of British families in HK. **Not a UK franchise** but follows the British Independent School model. PIS status.
- **Campuses:** Pok Fu Lam (Prep + main) + Kowloon Bay (secondary).
- **Curriculum:** English National Curriculum → IGCSE → **A-Level** (new university-style Sixth Form Centre opening Aug 2026 in addition to existing IB DP).
- **Tuition (2025/26):** Prep HK$208,800; Y7–11 HK$259,600; Y12–13 HK$267,100.
- **Capital Levy:** ACL HK$40K.
- **Debenture:** **Golden Jubilee Debenture HK$20M** (set the HK ceiling); other debenture tiers down to ~HK$2M.
- **Outcomes:** Strong UK university pipeline; mature, established alumni network.
- **Philosophy:** Very British, very community-feeling. Mature parent body. Less hard-edged than Harrow.
- **Math/Science depth:** Standard British school; Sixth Form Centre will increase A-Level subject breadth from 2026.
- **Mandarin:** Taught; not bilingual.
- **Mainland-background family fit:** Welcoming; smaller mainland-heritage cohort than Harrow but growing.
- **Honest weakness:** Top-of-market capital costs; debenture market has softened making the headline HK$20M figure increasingly notional.

### Nord Anglia International School Hong Kong (NAIS HK)
- **Founded:** 2014 as part of UK Nord Anglia Education group (80+ schools globally). Lam Tin + Kwun Tong campuses.
- **Curriculum:** Cambridge IGCSE → IB DP dual pathway.
- **Tuition (2025/26):** HK$91,300–222,500. **New Annual Capital Levy HK$35,000 from 2026/27** (replaces previous Capital Enrolment Fee).
- **Outcomes:** 2025 IB Avg 35.4.
- **Philosophy:** Modern, tech-forward. **MIT partnership** for STEAM curriculum; **Juilliard** for performing arts. Network exchanges across NAE schools globally.
- **Math/Science depth:** STEAM identity is real (MIT-developed challenges, robotics, coding) but IB averages are middle-tier.
- **Mandarin:** Putonghua taught.
- **Mainland-background family fit:** Welcoming; Nord Anglia brand strong in PRC.

### Stamford American School Hong Kong (SAIS)
- **Founded:** 2017. Cognita group (also runs Stamford American Singapore). Ho Man Tin + new West Kowloon HS campus opening 2026.
- **Curriculum:** American AERO/Common Core + IB DP option. **STEMinn** programme from age 5.
- **Tuition (2025/26):** HK$217,100–264,000.
- **Capital Levy:** HK$150K one-off OR HK$30K/yr.
- **Outcomes:** Newer; first IB cohorts emerging.
- **Philosophy:** STEM-forward, inquiry, American diversity.
- **Math/Science depth:** STEMinn is genuinely interesting; track record still developing.
- **Mandarin:** Daily mandatory Mandarin K–G5.
- **Mainland-background family fit:** Welcoming; capacity exists.

## The UK boarding-school wave — what's happening

Since 2012 there has been a **deliberate strategy by famous UK boarding schools to franchise into Asia**, especially HK, Bangkok, Beijing, Shanghai, Singapore, Dubai. Key drivers:
- HK middle/upper-class demand for "British prestige" education without sending children to UK at age 11–13.
- Mainland Chinese families seeing HK as a stepping stone to UK universities; preferring branded UK schools.
- UK schools needing additional revenue post-Brexit and post-tax-changes on UK private school fees.

This wave is still active: in addition to Harrow (2012), Malvern (2018), Shrewsbury (2018), and Mount Kelly (2017), more brands have explored HK or chosen alternative Asian markets. The HK supply has grown faster than demand in some segments — leading to the **collapse in international-school debenture secondary market prices** since 2020.

For families today this means:
- **More choice** than 5 years ago.
- **Less pressure** at the entry point of newer schools (vs CIS / HKIS / KGV).
- **Debentures are riskier as financial assets** — many families now prefer schools that charge an annual capital levy rather than ask for a multi-million debenture.
- **Capital costs ceiling has shifted from ~HK$5M (a decade ago) to HK$15M–20M** (Kellett Golden Jubilee, CIS Corporate Nomination Right). Critics say this prices out the diverse middle.

## Honest assessment for this family's brief

The family's brief: strong academics + confidence + competitive, **anti-rote**, math/physics, independent thinking, 2035–2050 lens.

**Best fit in this category:**
- **Harrow HK** if family is OK with Tuen Mun and traditional structure — the A-Level Maths + Further Maths + Physics + EPQ combo can deliver world-class STEM outcomes if the child opts in. Boarding option useful for HK/mainland mobility.
- **Malvern College HK** for a balance of British identity + IB inquiry. Tai Po location.

**Possible:**
- **Nord Anglia** if STEAM + MIT branding + lower fee tier appeals.
- **Stamford** if AP / IB STEM and smaller, growing-school feel works.

**Less compelling for this family:**
- **Mount Kelly** — too early in the build-out (no senior school yet).
- **Shrewsbury** — wait-and-see for the secondary track record.

**Wider point:** these schools tend to be **traditional / structured** by design. Families specifically wanting a less-pressurised, project-based environment may find ESF (RCHK, DC) or HKA more aligned, even if the prestige differs.
