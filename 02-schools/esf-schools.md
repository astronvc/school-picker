# English Schools Foundation (ESF)

The ESF is Hong Kong's largest international school operator, running **22 schools** including 9 primaries, 5 secondaries, 2 all-through schools (Discovery College and Renaissance College), 5 kindergartens and one special-needs school. ESF began as a colonial-government provision for English-speaking children of expatriates in 1967 (KGV in particular dates to 1894 as Central British School, becoming Quarry Bay primary in 1936 / re-founded as KGV 1948 — folded into ESF in 1981).

ESF historically received a HK government subsidy, but this was phased out for new students by 2016, making ESF effectively a private not-for-profit operator. Fees are now substantially higher than they once were but still below most other top-tier international schools.

## Why ESF matters for this family

- All 7 academic schools (5 secondaries + 2 all-through) are **IB DP authorised**; primaries follow IB PYP. Secondaries follow IB MYP-influenced model in Y7–10, IGCSE in Y11, IB Diploma in Y12–13. Renaissance College is full IB Continuum.
- ESF is generally regarded as **less academically pressurised than CIS or HKIS, but more so than HKA**. Increasingly competitive at admissions.
- 2025 IB results: 936 students sat exam, **avg 36.1**, 97.6% pass rate, **15 students achieved perfect 45**.
- Fits the brief of "rigour without 填鸭式" reasonably well, although STEM depth varies by school.

## Curriculum framework

- **Primary (PYP):** Inquiry-based, transdisciplinary, 6 themes/year. Mandarin offered (Putonghua).
- **Y7–10 (informal MYP-influenced):** Broad subject base.
- **Y11 (IGCSE):** Cambridge IGCSE.
- **Y12–13:** **IB Diploma Programme** is universal at all 5 ESF secondaries + DC + RCHK. Some schools (Island, SIS, RCHK) also offer the IB Career-related Programme (CP).

## Admissions process (2026)

1. **Zoning has been removed.** Families may apply to any ESF primary or secondary, no longer limited by catchment. (You can still preference your closest school, plus DC and/or RCHK; a 4th out-of-zone choice is allowed if you select your zoned school first.)
2. **Application window:** September the year before for Year 1 / Year 7 Central Application. K1/K2 separate timelines.
3. **Application fee 2026:** HK$2,800 per applicant (primary/secondary); HK$1,500 K1/K2 (revised from Sept 2026 user-pays model).
4. **Assessment:** Year 1 — playgroup-style observation; Year 7+ — written + interview.
5. **Priority order for offers:**
   1. Children of full-time ESF staff
   2. **Corporate Nomination Right (CNR)** holders
   3. Foundation Partners' nominees
   4. Siblings of current ESF students
   5. **Individual Nomination Right (INR)** holders
   6. Standard applicants
6. **Nomination Rights pricing (2026):**
   - INR: **HK$500,000** for ESF primary/secondary; **HK$400,000** for DC and RCHK.
   - CNR: substantially higher (corporate purchase only); first priority after staff children.
   - INR is one-time, transferable to siblings, no refund.

## ESF outcomes (2025 IB)

- ESF group: **avg 36.1; pass rate 97.6%; 15 perfect 45-scorers; 27 at 44 points**.
- Top 30-university destinations: 118 ESF students went to a global top-30 university in 2024 (12.1% of cohort) including 5 to Oxford, 14 to Imperial, 5 to UC Berkeley.
- Most popular destinations: UK (33.5%), HK (30.7%), then USA, Australia, Canada.

## Where ESF fits — honest assessment

### Strengths
- **Lower-pressure, inquiry-driven** culture relative to CIS or HKIS — closer to the family's "no 填鸭式" goal.
- **Lower fees** than CIS/HKIS/Harrow (~HK$140K–215K vs HK$220K–290K).
- **No mandatory debenture** — INR (HK$400K–500K) is optional, gives priority but isn't a barrier to entry on assessment alone.
- Universal IB pathway — globally recognised; encourages broad, balanced learning incl. CAS (creativity-action-service), TOK (theory of knowledge), Extended Essay (research project).
- **Renaissance College and Discovery College** offer **full IB Continuum from Y1** — strongest 12-year inquiry pipeline in HK.

### Weaknesses
- IB averages (35–38) are **below CIS / GSIS / SPCC / DBS / Po Leung Kuk Ngan Po Ling** — ESF is solid but not at the very top end of academic outcomes.
- **STEM depth is variable** — KGV and Island have strong physics/maths cohorts, but the IB Math AA HL is not as ubiquitous as at GSIS / DBS / SIS.
- **Less Mandarin emphasis** than CIS / ISF / VSA / SIS. Mandarin is taught but ESF is not bilingual.
- ESF can feel **dilute / large** — KGV alone has 1,800+ students; community feel varies.
- **Increasingly competitive** — ESF is no longer a "default" for HK English-speaking expats; sibling priority + nomination rights mean unfilled places at top schools are limited.

### Best ESF schools for the family's brief
- **STEM + structure:** KGV (largest math/sciences cohort), Island School (strong CP option for design/STEM), Sha Tin College (highest 2025 outcomes).
- **Through-train inquiry pipeline:** Discovery College or Renaissance College — full IB Continuum eliminates Y6→Y7 transition risk.
- **Mandarin support:** All ESF schools teach Mandarin; **Renaissance College** has the strongest Chinese programme of the all-through ESF schools.
